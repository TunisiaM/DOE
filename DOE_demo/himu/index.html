Overview

By the year 2025, all NYC public school students will receive meaningful, high‐quality Computer Science (CS) education at each school level: elementary, middle, and high school. Over the next 10 years, the DOE will train nearly 5,000 teachers who will bring CS education to the City’s ~1.1 million public school students. CS intersects many categories: Robotics, Makers, Mobile App Development, etc. To aid us with this quest, today we're going to have a Web Design and Development demo that will teach you some html basics and give you a list of resources you can use moving forward.

Objectives:

Teachers will be able to:

Understand what Computer Science Is

Learn HTML Fundamentals

Create an HTML Page

So we have our basic website, but there are a few key elements missing for this to be accurate. Let's go back to our sandwich.

For a sandwich, we always have two pieces of bread... but what about the stuff inside? What if I wanted a burger, with lettuce and tomatoes?

In an HTML document, we have two main elements that are always there:


head tag

body tag


Your head element contains all the non-visual information not seen within your webpage and/or browser. This is extremely important when it comes to the computer language CSS and learning how to style your html pages.

Your body tag contains all the visual content within your webpage.
